About

Theory meets
real-world engineering

The Cryptography & Distributed Systems Laboratory (CCSD) is an independent research center dedicated to advancing the mathematical and engineering foundations of secure, decentralized computation.

We bridge rigorous cryptographic theory with practical systems engineering — building open protocols, verifiable proofs, and tools that matter. Our work is public, reproducible, and grounded in formal correctness.

We collaborate with universities, open-source communities, and industry partners to ensure our research has lasting impact.

Research areas

What we work on

From zero-knowledge proofs to consensus protocols — our work spans the full stack of trustworthy distributed computation.

01
Zero-Knowledge Proofs
Designing and optimizing zkSNARK and zkSTARK systems for efficient proof generation and verification in constrained environments.
STARKs Plonk FRI
02
Consensus Protocols
Byzantine fault-tolerant consensus mechanisms with provable safety and liveness guarantees for large-scale distributed networks.
BFT PoS DAGs
03
Formal Verification
Machine-verified proofs of correctness for cryptographic protocols and smart contracts using interactive theorem provers.
Lean 4 Coq TLA+
04
Privacy-Preserving Computation
Multi-party computation, homomorphic encryption, and secure aggregation for private analytics and inference.
MPC FHE SMPC
05
Blockchain Scalability
Layer-2 architectures, rollup designs, and sharding schemes that preserve decentralization and cryptographic security.
Rollups Sharding Bridges
06
Cryptographic Primitives
New hash functions, elliptic curve constructions, and algebraic structures designed for zero-knowledge and recursive proof systems.
Poseidon KZG Curves
